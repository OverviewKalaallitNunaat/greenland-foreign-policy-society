1. Purpose

Nunanut Allanut Politikkeqarnermut Peqatigiiffik (Nunanut), internationally known as the Greenland Foreign Policy Society, is a voluntary association with the aim of promoting knowledge of and increasing interest in foreign policy issues in Greenland.

The association is independent of public, political, commercial, and organizational interests. It does not take positions on political issues but serves exclusively as a platform for information, debate, and networking.

2. Place of Residence

2.1

The association is based in Greenland, specifically within the Sermersooq municipality.

2.2

Activities primarily occur in Nuuk, the capital city of Sermersooq.

2.3

Events may also take place outside municipal boundaries due to international collaboration.

3. Members

3.1

Membership is open to all interested parties.

3.2

Residency within the geographical seat is not required for membership.

3.3

Termination of membership must be submitted in writing to the board and takes effect at the end of September.

4. Admission

4.1

The board may refuse membership applications at its discretion.

5. Membership Fee

5.1

Members pay an annual fee of DKK 50, collected in September.

5.6

Members enrolled in educational institutions are exempt from the fee.

5.7

Payment details: Bank of Greenland, Reg: 6471, Acc: 5906366.

6. General Meeting

6.1

The general meeting is the supreme authority of the association.

6.3

The board provides notice of the general meeting 14 days in advance via digital communication.

6.4

Only members with at least 6 months of membership are eligible to vote.

6.5

Members with outstanding dues are not eligible to vote.

7. Board of Directors

7.1

The board consists of the chairman, vice-chairman, and treasurer, elected for a one-year term.

7.2

Founding members of the board are permanent, subject to exclusion only under circumstances of negligence.

7.3

Founding members may resign upon notification to the board chairman or vice-chairman.

8. Economy

8.1

The financial year follows the calendar year.

8.2

All financial transactions require approval from both the chairman and the treasurer.

9. Liability

9.1

The association is liable only with its assets and does not impose personal liability on board members.

10. Amendments

10.1

The board may amend the articles during the first year following the association's establishment.

11. Termination

11.1

Proposals for dissolution must be initiated by the board and require a 2/3 majority at the general meeting.
