The Future of Arctic Trade Routes

By Malik Peter Koch Hansen • 16 January 2026

As the polar ice caps recede, the North Side of Greenland is transitioning from a frozen frontier to a central artery of global commerce.

The transformation of Arctic trade routes represents one of the most significant shifts in maritime history. Today, they are becoming viable alternatives to the Suez Canal.

For Greenland, this shift is not merely economic, it is foundational to our future foreign policy. Increased traffic brings a need for enhanced search and rescue capabilities and stricter environmental oversigth and regulations.
